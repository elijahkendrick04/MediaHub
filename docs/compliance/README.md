# docs/compliance

The data-protection paperwork for MediaHub. In plain words: MediaHub handles
swimmers' names, ages and photos — many of them children — so the law (UK GDPR
and friends) says we must know exactly what data we hold, why we're allowed to
hold it, and how a swimmer or parent can say "stop". These documents are that
proof, written so a regulator or a club committee could read them.

Everything legal-shaped in here is marked **DRAFT — FOR LEGAL REVIEW**: an
engineer wrote it from primary sources, and a solicitor must confirm it before
anyone treats it as final.

- [`LEGAL_FRAMEWORK.md`](LEGAL_FRAMEWORK.md) — the law as it stands (UK GDPR,
  DPA 2018, PECR, the Data (Use and Access) Act 2025 changes, the ICO
  Children's Code, EU rules, and international data transfers), verified
  against primary sources, with what each rule means for MediaHub.
- [`OPEN_LEGAL_QUESTIONS.md`](OPEN_LEGAL_QUESTIONS.md) — every judgment call
  we have NOT made: the question, the safe default we built, and a slot for
  the human/solicitor answer. Nothing legal is decided silently.

More documents land here as the compliance programme progresses (data map,
record of processing, gap analysis, privacy-notice templates, DPIA, breach
playbook).
