# ticker_strip

A broadcast-styled card built as a stack of full-width **horizontal bands** rather than a left/right or centred composition. A thin **top rail** on the surface colour carries the club at left and the meet at right like a scoreboard header; a dominant **name band** on the brand ground holds an accent achievement kicker, the first name in the accent colour, and an oversized full-width autofit surname; a **scoreline band** on the surface colour — fenced by accent rules top and bottom, with the event labelled at left and a monospaced result value boxed at right on the brand ground — gives the card its "ticker" signature; and a **foot band** on the brand ground seats the club lockup beside an optional highlight chip that collapses when empty. Its structural signature is that run of stacked full-bleed bands plus the fenced left-label / right-value scoreline, which reads at a glance as a different card from the centred numeral of `big_number_dominant`, the angled wedge of `split_diagonal_hero`, the vertical scoreboard rail of `stat_stack_sidebar`, and the flat type of `minimal_type_poster`. Because the name band flexes while the rails, scoreline, and foot keep their intrinsic height, the same composition holds at both 1080×1350 and 1080×1920. **The director should pick this archetype for a single result where the broadcast/scoreboard framing fits** — a clear event→result pairing worth presenting as a scoreline, especially for finals, qualifying times, and record swims — and it suits energetic, "matchday" moods. It needs no photo and works as a clean type-and-data card; a supplied portrait sits as a quiet desaturated wash behind the name band rather than as the focal element (prefer a photo-led archetype when the imagery should dominate).
