# duo_athlete_split

A matchday **duel poster**. The canvas is bisected into two equal vertical
halves by a hard accent seam down the centre line: the left half is the
**photo bay** (the athlete in the water, with the achievement kicker chipped
top-left and the sponsor low in the frame), and the right half is a solid
brand **data bay** — the photo's typographic twin — with the club lockup
anchored top-right and a scoreline ledger (event, result in oversized
monospace accent, optional highlight) anchored bottom-right. One full-width
**name band** crosses both halves at the vertical midpoint — the only element
allowed to bridge the seam — so the athlete's name literally ties the swim
and the scoreline together. The structural signature is the 50/50 vertical
bisection crossed by one horizontal band (a "plus" of seam × band): unlike
the narrow off-centre rail of `stat_stack_sidebar`, the three bays of
`triptych_progression`, the stacked horizontal bands of `ticker_strip`, or
the angled horizontal split of `split_diagonal_hero`. Opposite-corner
anchoring (kicker top-left, ledger bottom-right) gives it diagonal tension
without an actual diagonal. With no photo the left bay falls back to painted
swim-lane ropes and an accent ring on the brand ground, so the duel structure
never looks broken.

**When the director should pick it:** head-to-head and "the swim vs. the
scoreline" moments — a tight final, a race decided on the touch, a
two-athlete or relay photo that fills a tall half-frame well (the bay crop is
a narrow portrait, so one or two bodies read better than a wide team shot),
or any strong single-athlete portrait where the result deserves equal billing
with the imagery rather than a caption strip. Suits "fierce", "electric" and
"precise" moods. Avoid when the only photo is a wide landscape team shot
(the half-frame crop will cut it badly — prefer `full_bleed_photo_lower_third`),
or when there is no result value to carry the data bay (prefer a type-led
archetype such as `minimal_type_poster` or `quote_led_recap`).
