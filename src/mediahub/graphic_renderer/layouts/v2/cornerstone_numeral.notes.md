# cornerstone_numeral

The result is laid as a **cornerstone**: one mega monospaced numeral pinned hard
to the bottom-left and reading up off the floor, with the athlete and brand
stacked along the top edge and the event sitting on the figure's baseline. The
upper field is left deliberately open, so the weight sits in one corner rather
than floating dead-centre. The bottom-left corner-anchored figure is the
structural signature — a different silhouette at a glance from the centred big
numeral, the lower-third band, or any photo wedge. Entirely typographic, so it
never depends on an image.

**When the director should pick it:** pure-stat moments where the figure is the
story — a sharp time, a big PB delta, a points or count total — and any high-rank
card with no usable photo, especially when you want a grounded, weighty,
poster-like feel rather than a symmetric centred lockup. Strongest for short
result values; suits "precise", "stoic", "bold" and data-led registers. Avoid
when the moment is about the person (use a photo-led or spotlight archetype) or
when the result value is missing.
