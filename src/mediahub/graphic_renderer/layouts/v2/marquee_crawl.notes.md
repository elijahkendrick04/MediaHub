# marquee_crawl

A stadium ribbon-board: several full-width horizontal marquee bands are stacked
top-to-bottom as the whole backdrop, each carrying the card's real facts repeated
across the width — label, surname, event, result and meet — as an over-scaled
uppercase ribbon. Over that wall of ribbons a solid centre plate is pinned
mid-frame, fenced by accent keylines top and bottom, holding the hero: the
achievement, the oversized surname, the verified result and the event. The plate is
opaque, so the busy backdrop never obscures the facts. The structural signature is
the multi-band repeating-text wall as the entire ground, punctuated by one pinned
plate. That sets it apart from ticker_strip, which is a single fenced scoreline band
in a structured header/name/foot stack; here the ribbon wall itself is the
composition and every band is the same repeated fact stream. It is fully
typographic, with no photo, and every ribbon carries only verified facts.

**When the director should pick it:** high-energy, broadcast-flavoured moments that
want a loud, kinetic stadium-ribbon backdrop while still locking one clear result on
a pinned plate — a marquee win, a big final, a hype-worthy headline time. It suits
bold, energetic and celebratory registers where the repetition of the athlete's name
and mark is the point. Avoid it for restrained or minimal club tones, for cards that
must present several distinct results, or when a strong single portrait should
carry the frame instead of a typographic wall.
