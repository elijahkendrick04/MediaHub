# big_number_dominant

The **result is the hero**: one enormous monospaced numeral spans almost the
full canvas width, vertically centred on a flat brand ground, with the athlete
and event reduced to a compact kicker block above and a brand ledger (logo,
club, meet, sponsor) below. A faint oversized ghost of the achievement word
sits behind the numeral for depth, and thin accent rules frame the data. The
structural signature is the single dominant numeral on a flat ground — no
photo stage, no bands, no grid — which reads at a glance as a different card
from the diagonal photo wedge, the lower-third, or any centred-portrait
layout. Entirely typographic, so it never depends on an image.

**When the director should pick it:** pure-stat moments where the number IS
the story — a sharp time, a big PB delta, a points total, a county/regional
qualifying time — and any high-rank card with no usable photo. Strongest for
short result values (times fit; long free-text results weaken it). Suits
"data-led", "precise" and "stoic" moods and sponsor-safe neutral registers.
Avoid when the moment is about the person rather than the figure (use a
photo-led or spotlight archetype), or when the result value is missing.
