# editorial_numbers_grid

A magazine **results page**: a ruled masthead carries the club lockup at left
and the meet at right over a heavy rule, the achievement kicker and athlete
name are set large beneath it, and the facts sit in a hairline-ruled **grid of
labelled stat cells** (Event / Result / Recognition), with an emphasis tag and
sponsor along the foot. Information-dense and entirely typographic on the deep
surface ground. The structural signature is the rectilinear ruled grid of
label+value cells under a masthead — the opposite of the photo-led wedge and
lower-third archetypes, and unlike the single-numeral or centred layouts: it
reads as a set table of facts, not a poster.

**When the director should pick it:** data-led recaps where several facts
deserve equal billing — a swim with a notable time *and* placing *and*
recognition tag, weekend round-ups condensed to one athlete's line, or
committee/sponsor-facing cards that should read as a formal results sheet. A
strong no-photo option and a natural pick for "precise", "stoic" and
sponsor-safe neutral tones. Avoid for emotional hero moments that want
imagery or one dominant number (use the photo stages or
`big_number_dominant`), and prefer short labels — very long event names wrap
but a grid full of long strings loses its tabular crispness.
