# spotlight_disc

A single circular portal holds the athlete, ringed by an accent band and centred
on a radially-lit ground; the kicker sits above the **disc** and the name plus
result read beneath it in a symmetric stack. The round photo well on a radial
glow is the structural signature — distinct from the rectangular medal spotlight
and from any full-frame stage. With no photo the disc stays a clean brand well
behind the accent ring, so the centred lockup still holds together.

**When the director should pick it:** singular, celebratory hero moments built
around one person — a standout swim, a debut, an athlete-of-the-meet — where a
focused, badge-like circular portrait feels right and the composition wants to be
symmetric and centred. Best with a tight head-and-shoulders photo; suits
"triumphant", "celebratory" and "bold" registers. Avoid for multi-athlete or
multi-stat moments (use a grid, triptych or scoreline) and when the photo is wide
action better served full-bleed.
