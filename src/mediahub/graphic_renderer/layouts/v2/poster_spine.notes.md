# poster_spine

**Family / structural signature.** The varsity/broadcast spine: the surname runs
full-height up the left rail in a rotated display cut (`writing-mode:vertical-rl`)
— the only archetype with a rotated hero — while the facts (kicker, first name,
event, mega result, club lockup) stack on the right in the usual horizontal
register, divided by an accent rule. The vertical name is sized by a
vertical-aware autofit so it fills the rail height without overflowing its width.
Type-led: no photo slot, the name is the artwork.

**When the director should pick it** for a stark, editorial type poster when the
surname is the whole story and a strong rotated axis suits the moment — a single
marquee swimmer, a champion, a season headline — and there is no photo (or the
photo is being saved for another card in the pack). Best with a short-to-medium
surname; a very long compound name still fits (the spine autofit shrinks it) but
reads strongest at display scale.
