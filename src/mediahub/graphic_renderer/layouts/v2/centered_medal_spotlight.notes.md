# centered_medal_spotlight

A symmetric, ceremonial composition built on a **centred axis**: the athlete
sits inside a circular brand spotlight ringed in accent (a medal/podium
visual metaphor), beneath an achievement kicker, with a centred name lockup,
the event, the result in a pill, and a centred club foot. A radial gradient
ground focuses light behind the ring. When no photo is supplied the ring
frames a bold monogram instead of a cutout, so the card never looks broken.
The structural signature is **radial symmetry** — the deliberate counterpoint
to every left-aligned, grid, band or split archetype in the library; nothing
else centres the whole composition.

**When the director should pick it:** medals, podium finishes, trophies,
finals wins, records and milestone celebrations — moments with a ceremonial,
"spotlight on one athlete" feel; it is the natural home for `medal` /
`trophy` focal elements and "celebratory" or "triumphant" moods. Works with
or without a photo (the ring crops a portrait tightly, so head-and-shoulders
images read best). Avoid for data-dense recaps or multi-stat cards (the
centred column holds only a few lines gracefully) and for wide action photos
that fight the circular crop.
