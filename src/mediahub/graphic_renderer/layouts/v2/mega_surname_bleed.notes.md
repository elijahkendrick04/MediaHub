# mega_surname_bleed

The surname is set enormous and **bleeds off the right edge** — an asymmetric type
poster where the name itself is the artwork. A tight left lockup (kicker, first
name, event, result chip and brand) reads as the caption to the oversized type.
The clipped, right-bleeding headline is the structural signature, distinct from
the centred or stacked minimal poster and from the dead-centre big numeral. Purely
typographic; it never needs a photo and is at its best on a strong, short surname.

**When the director should pick it:** name-led moments where the athlete is the
headline — a marquee performer, a star turn, a "remember this name" card — and you
want a bold, editorial, fashion-poster energy. Strongest for short, punchy
surnames; suits "bold", "fierce" and "electric" registers and sponsor-safe
type-only layouts. Avoid for very long or hyphenated surnames that lose impact
when clipped, for multi-athlete recaps, and when a photo should carry the card.
