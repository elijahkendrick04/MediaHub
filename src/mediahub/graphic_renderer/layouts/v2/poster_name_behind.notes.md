# poster_name_behind

**Family / structural signature.** The layered sports poster: a mega autofit
surname is set on the ground plane as the artwork, the athlete CUTOUT stands
over it — so the type reads through and around the silhouette, giving the card
real front-to-back depth — and a solid lower-third band rides over the
cutout's lower body carrying the event, the result chip and the club lockup.
The kicker and first name hold the top-left. The cutout carries a
role-coloured lift (soft shadow + faint accent glow, scaled by decoration
strength), so the subject visibly sits *in front of* the name rather than
pasted beside it. This is the only archetype where the athlete physically
overlaps the headline plane — distinct from mega_surname_bleed (pure type, no
photo) and from every rectangular object-fit photo window.

**When the director should pick it:** a strong single-subject cutout — a clean
matte of one swimmer, portrait or celebration — paired with a short, punchy
surname; hero moments (a big PB, a title, a headline swim) where the athlete
IS the story and deserves poster treatment. Skip it for photo-less cards
(mega_surname_bleed serves those), for busy multi-subject photos, and for very
long double-barrelled surnames where the name plane loses its impact.

Photo mode: **cutout** (the layering is the point). With no photo the mega
name is the artwork and the band still anchors the facts; when the matte gate
rejects a cutout the original photograph honestly renders as a full-bleed
stage under a brand scrim with the name above it.
