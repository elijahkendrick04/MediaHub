# scoreline_versus

A centred **scoreline**: the event on the left and the result on the right,
divided by a bold vertical fence down the middle, so the card reads like a
fixture or scoreboard line. The athlete name banners the top and the meet plus
brand close the foot. The mirror-symmetric left-versus-right split across a
central divider is the structural signature, distinct from the stacked ticker and
the single horizon band. Built to frame two facts against each other.

**When the director should pick it:** comparison-shaped moments — a relay or
split set against a target, a heat-versus-final, an event paired with its mark,
or any head-to-head where two figures deserve equal billing across a divider.
Reads with no photo and suits "precise", "data-led" and competitive registers.
Avoid when there is really only one number to show (the big numeral or horizon
band frames a single figure better) or when a photo should lead.
