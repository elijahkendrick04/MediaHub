# full_bleed_photo_lower_third

A broadcast **lower third**: the athlete fills a full-bleed photo stage over a
brand-gradient ground, with the achievement kicker chipped top-left and the
club lockup top-right; a solid brand band pinned to the bottom carries the
first name and a full-width autofit surname, then a meta row — event, meet and
highlight at left, the result isolated in an accent chip at right, sponsor
beneath. With no photo the stage stays a clean brand gradient (no floating
cutout), so the card still reads. The structural signature is the horizontal
bottom band with a full-width name and a right-hand result chip under open
imagery — instantly "sports TV graphic", unlike the diagonal wedge (angled
seam), the ticker (multiple stacked bands) or any centred or grid layout.

**When the director should pick it:** the default photo-led hero when there is
one strong action or portrait image and a single headline fact — race wins,
debut swims, galas with great photography — where the imagery should breathe
and the data should sit quietly beneath it. The full-bleed stage is the most
forgiving crop in the library (wide or tall photos both work), so prefer it
over the split/duo archetypes when photo composition is unknown. Suits
"explosive", "electric" and "bold" moods. Avoid when there is no photo and
the moment is data-rich (a type-led or grid archetype shows the facts
better), or when several stats deserve equal billing — the band holds one
hero fact well, not four.
