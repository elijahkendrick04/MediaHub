# stat_stack_sidebar

A two-zone card split vertically: a wide left **stage** on the brand ground carries the headline — an accent kicker with a tick, the athlete's first name in the accent colour, an oversized autofit surname, then a result ledger pairing the event with a monospaced result value — while a narrow **right rail** runs the full height as a surface-coloured "scoreboard" strip, divided from the stage by a bold accent seam. The rail stacks short meta top-to-bottom (the meet, an optional emphasis/highlight chip that collapses when empty) and anchors the club lockup at its foot. Its structural signature is the persistent vertical surface rail down the right edge with stacked label/value chips plus the seam, which reads at a glance as a different card from the centred numeral of `big_number_dominant`, the angled wedge of `split_diagonal_hero`, and the flat type of `minimal_type_poster`. Because the rail content is bottom-anchored and the ledger is vertically centred in the stage, the same composition holds at both 1080×1350 and 1080×1920 (the stage simply grows taller). **The director should pick this archetype for a single-athlete result where there is meaningful context to surface alongside the headline** — a meet name plus an emphasis stat (a PB delta, a placing, a split) — and it suits composed, "confident" moods. It needs no photo and works as a clean type-and-data card; if a portrait is supplied it sits as a quiet desaturated band behind the stage headline rather than as the focal element (prefer a photo-led archetype when the imagery should dominate).
