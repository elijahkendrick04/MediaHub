# vertical_split

The whole frame is cut top-to-bottom into two solid colour fields that meet at a
horizontal seam a touch above centre. The athlete identity — achievement chip,
first name and an oversized surname — lives in the upper field (laid over the
athlete's photo when one is supplied, scrimmed to its lower edge so the name stays
legible), and the verified result owns the lower field on the surface colour, with
a lit accent seam rule fencing the two halves. The structural signature is the two
full-bleed solid colour fields — brand ground above, surface below — meeting at one
accent seam, with WHO above the line and WHAT below it. That sets it apart from
horizon_band, where a thin accent band merely rides on a single ground; here each
half is its own colour field. It reads with or without a photo.

**When the director should pick it:** clean, confident single-result moments where
you want a decisive top/bottom division between the athlete and their mark — a
final time, a winning result, a headline PB — and a calm split of identity above
from result below. It suits bold, precise and celebratory-but-tidy registers, works
equally with a strong portrait in the upper field or as pure type, and shines when
the result is the whole story. Avoid it when several competing numbers must share
the card (reach for the numbers grid or the scoreline) or when the moment needs a
busier, more editorial backdrop.
