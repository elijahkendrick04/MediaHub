# quote_led_recap

An editorial **pull-quote recap**. The card is composed like a magazine
blockquote: a light **surface** "paper" ground, a thick accent quote-bar down
the left margin, an oversized hanging quotation glyph, and the athlete set as the
pulled subject in left-aligned ragged display type (first name as the accent
lead-in, surname huge). The result is pinned as an editorial **margin-stat** on
the right behind a divider, with the hero stat as a quiet sub-note, and a
journalistic **em-dash attribution line** under a hairline rule carries the club
lockup (the "masthead") and sponsor. The distinctiveness is the blockquote
*furniture* (light editorial ground + left quote-bar + hanging quote glyph +
ragged left column + bottom attribution rule), not a recolour, so it reads as a
journalistic recap quote at a glance — unlike the centred numeral, the lower-third
band, the angled wedge, the vertical scoreboard rail, the broadcast bands, the
three vertical bays, the magazine cover, or the dark-ground centred type poster.
It is purely typographic (no photo required), so it is also a strong pick when no
portrait is available.

**When the director should pick it:** narrative / feature / "verdict" recaps where
the moment wants an editorial, reflective tone rather than a scoreboard — a
season-defining swim written up as a story, a profile or milestone post, or any
card with no usable portrait that should still feel considered. Prefer it when the
result is a single short headline value (a time or place) suited to the margin
stat and the club name is short enough to read as a masthead. Avoid for
multi-athlete relays (use a split/duo archetype) or dense multi-result recaps (use
the numbers grid / sidebar).
