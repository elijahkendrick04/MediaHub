# split_diagonal_hero

A hard diagonal cleaves the canvas into two zones: an upper full-bleed **photo stage** (the athlete in the moment) and a lower solid **brand wedge** that carries every fact, separated by a thin accent seam running along the diagonal. The wedge holds a left-aligned ledger — an achievement kicker with an accent tick, the athlete's first name in the accent colour, an oversized surname as the hero, the event, then a monospaced result value alongside an emphasis stat over an outline rule, with the brand lockup and an optional sponsor block along the foot. Its structural signature is the angled seam plus the asymmetric, fully left-aligned solid wedge, which reads at a glance as a different card from `big_number_hero` (a centred numeral) and `individual_hero` (a floating cutout over a gradient with the surname behind). Because the wedge is bottom-anchored at a fixed content height while the photo stage fills everything above it, the same composition holds at both 1080×1350 (photo ≈ half the frame) and 1080×1920 (the photo grows tall above an unchanged data band). **The director should pick this archetype when there is a strong full-frame action or portrait photo of a single athlete and one headline achievement** (a PB, a first-time, a standout swim) where the imagery should dominate while still presenting one or two hard facts — the result plus an emphasis stat such as a PB delta — and it suits energetic, "explosive" moods. It degrades to a brand-surface colour field if no photo is supplied, but when imagery is weak the director should prefer a type-led archetype instead.
