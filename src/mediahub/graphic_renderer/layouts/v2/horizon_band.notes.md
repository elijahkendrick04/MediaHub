# horizon_band

A single thick accent **horizon** runs across the vertical middle and carries the
result on it; the athlete name sits in the "sky" above the line and the event,
meet and brand sit on the "ground" below it. The whole card organises around one
strong horizontal axis, so the eye lands on the band and the result it holds. The
single full-width horizon carrying the figure is the structural signature —
distinct from the ticker's stack of broadcast bands and from the lower-third band
pinned to the floor. Fully typographic; reads with or without a photo.

**When the director should pick it:** clean, confident result moments where you
want one bold horizontal beat — a final time, a winning mark, a headline stat —
and a calm split between who (above) and what (below). Works with no photo and
suits "bold", "precise" and celebratory-but-tidy registers. Avoid when there are
several competing numbers to show at once (use the numbers grid or the scoreline)
or when the moment is carried by a strong photo.
