# band_break

**Family / structural signature.** A broadcast lower third, broken by the
athlete: a solid brand band carries the first name, an oversized surname, the
event/meet meta and the result chip across the lower third of the frame — and
the athlete cutout's head and shoulders visibly OVERLAP the band's top edge,
standing in front of the furniture. Pure z-index layering over one shared
cutout (body plane under the band, a clipped head/shoulders plane over it),
with the band's top edge placed from the cutout's alpha bounding box so the
break always lands at the shoulders, never through a face. A faint surname
watermark holds the ground behind the subject, and the cutout carries the
role-coloured depth lift. Distinct from full_bleed_photo_lower_third (band
over an uninterrupted photo stage) and from poster_name_behind (type plane
behind the athlete, no band break).

**When the director should pick it:** a clean single-subject cutout —
head-and-torso or full-body — on a result-led moment where the name band
should dominate but the card still deserves physical depth: finals wins,
podium swims, senior spotlights. Skip it for photo-less cards, group shots,
and mattes where the head sits low in frame (the break needs headroom above
the band).

Photo mode: **cutout** (the band break IS the layering). With no photo the
band + watermark read as a clean lower third; when the matte gate rejects a
cutout the original photograph honestly renders as the classic full-bleed
stage under the band, no break.
