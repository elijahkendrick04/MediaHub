# minimal_type_poster

A purely typographic **poster** — no photo, ever. The achievement kicker and
meet share a quiet top rail; the first name leads into an enormous stacked
surname (the mega autofit slot) on a flat brand ground; a single accent rule
splits the canvas; and the event/result sit as a restrained baseline ledger
with the brand foot below. Generous negative space everywhere. The structural
signature is huge stacked type + one rule + flat ground — deliberate
minimalism against the busier archetypes; nothing else in the library trades
this much canvas for silence.

**When the director should pick it:** name-first moments — a swimmer's debut,
a captaincy or selection announcement, a "first ever" milestone, or any
high-rank card with no usable image where the *person* (not the number)
should dominate. The aesthetic is premium and restrained, so it suits
"minimal", "calm" and "stoic" moods, brand kits with strong colour identity,
and clubs that want an editorial feel. It is also the cleanest fallback when
photo quality is poor. Avoid for data-dense recaps (one event + one result is
its comfortable maximum) and for celebratory moments that want imagery,
medals or energy — the restraint reads cold for those.
