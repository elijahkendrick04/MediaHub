# broadcast_scorebug

A live-television treatment: the athlete fills a full-bleed photo stage, a thin
top ribbon carries club and meet like an on-air banner, and a compact
**scorebug** sits in the lower-left corner — a boxed event-to-result cluster with
an accent tab, exactly like a broadcast lower-corner graphic. The corner bug
module rather than a full-width band is the structural signature, distinct from
the lower-third archetype. With no photo the stage falls back to a clean brand
gradient so the bug still reads.

**When the director should pick it:** energetic, in-the-moment photo cards that
should feel like live coverage — a strong action shot or poolside portrait where
a tidy, broadcast-style data bug adds credibility without covering the image.
Best with a real athlete photo and a short result; suits "electric", "explosive"
and hype registers. Avoid when there is no usable photo (use a data-led
archetype) or when a calmer, editorial feel is wanted.
