# magazine_cover

A sports-magazine **cover** composition: a top masthead rail (club as the
title, meet as the issue/dateline) over a full-bleed portrait "cover well", with
an oversized cover-headline surname bleeding up from the lower-left over the
photo, a vertical column of "cover lines" (event, hero stat) pinned down the
right margin, a circular accent **coverstar** burst carrying the headline result
in the lower-right, and a foot strip with the club lockup and a faux barcode. The
distinctiveness is the cover *furniture* (masthead + headline-over-photo +
marginal cover-line column + coverstar burst), not a recolour, so it reads as a
magazine cover at a glance — unlike the centred numeral, the lower-third band,
the angled wedge, the vertical rail, the broadcast bands or the three vertical
bays. When no portrait is supplied the well degrades to a solid brand-ground
field so the headline and coverstar still compose.

**When the director should pick it:** marquee single-athlete moments that warrant
a "cover star" treatment — a standout PB, a champion swim, a profile/feature post
— especially when a strong portrait is available. Prefer it when the result is a
single short headline value (a time or place) that suits the coverstar burst, and
when the club name is short enough to read as a masthead. Avoid for multi-athlete
relays (use a split/duo archetype) or data-dense recaps (use the numbers grid /
sidebar).
