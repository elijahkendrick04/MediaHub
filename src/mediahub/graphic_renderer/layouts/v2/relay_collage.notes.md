# relay_collage

A **squad / relay poster**. The upper stage is a shared studio backdrop on the
brand ground where two to four athlete cutouts stand together on one baseline —
a deterministically balanced group, weighted so the composition never lists to
one side, with the outermost bodies turned inward and a soft contact shadow
grounding each one. A full-width accent **name band** crosses the foot of the
stage, and a solid **data panel** runs across the bottom carrying the event, the
result in oversized monospace, an optional highlight and the meet line. The
structural signature is a three-deck horizontal stack — multi-subject stage over
name band over data panel — built around a row of people sharing one floor.
Unlike `duo_athlete_split`, which bisects the canvas into a single photo bay and
a scoreline twin, this archetype is the only one that *composites several
cutouts into one frame*; unlike the three text bays of `triptych_progression`
or the narrow rail of `stat_stack_sidebar`, its hero is a group of bodies. When
only one — or no — usable photo resolves, the stage keeps the single athlete
photo cover-cropped on its saliency focus over the painted backdrop, so the
deck structure never looks broken.

**When the director should pick it:** team and squad moments where the people,
plural, are the story — a relay (the classic four-swimmer medley or freestyle
team), a medal-winning quartet, a pair or trio of athletes who all hit a PB or
final in the same race, a club's "our weekend in one frame" group shot. It needs
two to four athlete or team cutouts to come alive; with a single portrait prefer
`duo_athlete_split` or `full_bleed_photo_lower_third`, and with no result value
to anchor the data panel prefer a type-led archetype such as
`minimal_type_poster`. Suits "together", "proud", "electric" and "celebratory"
moods.
