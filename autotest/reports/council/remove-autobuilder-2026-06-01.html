Where the council clashed

The roadmap badge-updater. First Principles said keep (CI documentation); Expansionist + Outsider said remove (it "becomes a liar" with nothing advancing the roadmap). Peer review demanded the deciding fact: who still writes the roadmap: trailers? Verified — after removal, nothing in autotest/ does (only the deleted builder/accept did; the fixer writes none). Chairman call: KEEP — it's a separate subsystem, two of its three functions are builder-independent and useful, and the operator scoped removal to the autobuilder of the roadmap tool, not the tool itself. One honest clarifying line removes the "ghost" smell.

Blind spots peer review caught

Naming — harness.py collides with "the harness"

The whole autotest/ package is colloquially "the harness" in CLAUDE.md. The new module is named gitops.py instead, to stay unambiguous.

Env vars — don't rename AUTOTEST_BUILD_MERGE

It's read by _merge_to_main, which the kept fixer workflow sets. Renaming would silently change fixer behaviour on the runner. All AUTOTEST_BUILD_* names are preserved verbatim.

Docs lie after removal

autotest/README.md (build-loop diagram, full-autonomy section), CLAUDE.md, and several docs/*.md describe a system that won't exist. Honest doc rewrite is in scope, not optional.

Process & reversibility

Run the CLAUDE.md 15-step breakage check first; one reviewable commit; verify the deterministic engine is untouched. "Fully remove from ever existing" settles reversibility — git history is the only hedge, no commented-out code.
