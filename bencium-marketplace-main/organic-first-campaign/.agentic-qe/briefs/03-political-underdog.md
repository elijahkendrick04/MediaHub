# Brief 03 — Regional political candidate, categorical asymmetry

**Outcome:** Win a regional mayoral race in a mid-sized city (population 180,000) against a 3-term incumbent backed by the ruling national party.

**Audience:** Registered voters in the city. Key segments: young professionals (25-40, returning from capital/abroad), small business owners, public-sector workers, retirees.

**Sector:** Political/civic, local government.

**Budget:** €25,000 total campaign budget over 6 months. Incumbent's campaign is reported at €1.2M+ with additional state-media coverage worth an estimated €3-5M in equivalent paid placement. Ratio in paid spend: roughly 1:200 not counting state media.

**Competition:** Incumbent has saturating presence on state TV, regional billboards, Facebook ads in the local feed, and sponsored newspaper supplements. Messaging is stability, continuity, delivered infrastructure projects.

**Existing channels:** Candidate has 12k Facebook followers (built over 2 years of city-council work), an active local Discord for young professionals (800 members), and strong relationships with 5 independent local Facebook groups (total reach ~45k). Recently completed 60 small in-person meetings across neighborhoods.

**Bottleneck:** Visibility without matching the incumbent's frequency. Trust is strong in the existing base but unknown beyond it. Local media is partially captured.

**Time window:** 6 months to election day.

**Capacity:** Candidate + campaign manager + 2 paid staff + ~30 active volunteers. Volunteers: ~4 hrs/week each average. Core team: 40+ hrs/week each.
