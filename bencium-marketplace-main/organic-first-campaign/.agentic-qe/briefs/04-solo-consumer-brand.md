# Brief 04 — Solo consumer brand, severe asymmetry

**Outcome:** Hit €15,000/month recurring revenue in 9 months for a direct-to-consumer natural skincare line (3 SKUs currently, €35-45 price points).

**Audience:** Women 30-50 with sensitive skin, interested in clean/simple ingredient lists, willing to pay premium, active on Instagram and newsletter-reading.

**Sector:** Consumer brand, personal care.

**Budget:** €2,000/month total. Currently €800 on Instagram ads, the rest on product and shipping. Against major brands in the category spending €50k+/month on paid social in this region. Ratio ~1:60.

**Competition:** Established clean-beauty brands with strong Instagram presence, influencer partnerships (budgets €2-5k per post for mid-tier), and shelf distribution. Messaging: science-backed, dermatologist-approved, Instagram-aesthetic packaging.

**Existing channels:** Founder's personal Instagram with 2,800 followers (built over 18 months of posting her own skincare journey). 420 email subscribers, 28% open rate. No paid creators, no retail, no wholesale. She's the face of the brand.

**Bottleneck:** The category is saturated with authenticity claims. Customers don't believe founder stories anymore because everyone has one. Acquisition cost on paid Instagram is running at €48, AOV is €67, margin after COGS/shipping is €22. Math doesn't work on paid.

**Time window:** 9 months. After that, runway is a question.

**Capacity:** Just the founder. 10-12 hrs/week on marketing (the rest is fulfillment, customer service, formulation).
