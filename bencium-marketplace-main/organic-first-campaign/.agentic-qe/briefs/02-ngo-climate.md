# Brief 02 — Climate NGO, severe asymmetry

**Outcome:** Mobilize 5,000 verified supporters (donation or signed petition) in Central Europe over the next 4 months to pressure a specific policy vote.

**Audience:** Climate-concerned adults 25-55 in Hungary, Poland, Czechia, Slovakia. Readers of centre-left press, university graduates, professionals. Not yet active donors to climate causes.

**Sector:** Environmental NGO, policy advocacy.

**Budget:** €12,000 total for the 4 months. Currently 40% earmarked for Facebook/Instagram ads by the board. Ratio vs. the fossil-fuel-lobby-linked opposition campaign is roughly 1:30.

**Competition:** Well-funded industry-front groups running slick Facebook ad campaigns. Known messaging frames ("affordable energy", "energy sovereignty"). Opposition has paid influencer relationships with 4 named mid-tier creators.

**Existing channels:** NGO has 8,000 email subscribers (opens ~12%), 2,400 Instagram followers, 1 volunteer coordinator running regional meetups in Budapest and Krakow. Partnership with 1 respected investigative journalism outlet (Direkt36-tier).

**Bottleneck:** Trust. The audience is skeptical of NGOs in general after recent greenwashing scandals. They believe the problem but not the messengers. Paid ads have been tried (€4k last year) and converted poorly.

**Time window:** Policy vote in 4 months. Hard deadline.

**Capacity:** 3 FTE campaigners, 2 comms staff at 50% capacity on this. ~120 hrs/week total team capacity.
